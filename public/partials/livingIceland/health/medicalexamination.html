Applicants for a residence permit coming from Central and South America, including Mexico, non-EEA European countries, Asia or Africa must undergo a medical examination for infectious diseases. If the applicant, in the opinion of a physician, has a valid medical certificate from abroad, which has been issued less than three months before, the applicant concerned does not have to undergo a medical examination. Those coming from the EEA (other than Romania and Bulgaria), Australia, New Zealand, Switzerland, the USA and Canada do not have to submit such a medical certificate. For more information please see the Directorate of Health's website: www.landlaeknir.is

The medical examination for a residence permit is expensive. The costs of these medical tests varies, depending on where you are from (around ISK 20,000-30,000).

The medical examination procedure. (See the pdf document "Procedures for the Medical Examination of Immigrants to Iceland")

Reykjavík

Students needing a medical examination should visit the Division of Communicable Disease Control, at Álfabakki 16, 109 Reykjavík. The results of these tests are sent directly to the Immigration Office. You need to make an appointment by calling: +354 585 1390. For further information see the website of the Health Care Centres in Iceland or ask the international office at your university for advice.

Outside of Reykjavík

Those not living in the Reykjavík area may turn to their local health clinic for this examination. Please contact the international office at your university for information.
