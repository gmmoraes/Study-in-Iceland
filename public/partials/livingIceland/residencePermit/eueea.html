Citizens of the European Union and the European Economic Area planning to stay longer than three months in Iceland, need a residence permit. The permit may be obtained after arriving in Iceland from most of the EU and EEA countries. EU and EEA citizens do not need a special work permit, only a residence permit. A list of EEA countries at www.utl.is.

More information about residence permits for citizens of EEA member countries.

If you have any inquiries about the application process, please contact the Directorate of Immigration.

Nordic citizens

Citizens from Nordic countries planning to study in Iceland must register at the Registers Iceland (Þjóðskrá Íslands), Borgartún 21, 105 Reykjavík, tel. +354 515 5300, upon arrival in Iceland.

Nordic citizens do not need residence or work permits.

Upon arrival in Iceland all students must:

  • apply for a kennitala, an Icelandic ID number/social security number. This ID number is necessary for various practical matters such as opening a bank account, getting a tax card, etc.
  • register their domicile in Iceland
  • remember to bring their passport, residence permit (if applicable) and the acceptance letter from their university in Iceland when visiting the municipal office
